

The United States is increasing its aid to Jordan  by $100 million  to help the nation&rsquo;s poor, its young people and its underprivileged. The grant is in addition to the $363 million in regular  economic assistance to Jordan for the  2010 fiscal year. Jordan is grappling with rising  prices for basic commodities and fuel. This week, the government slashed prices, possibly fearing the type of unrest that has hit Tunisia and Algeria.
