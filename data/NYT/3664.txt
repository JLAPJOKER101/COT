

German opposition parties were unable to muster a majority to oppose the reported multibillion-dollar deal to sell 200 German-made tanks to Saudi Arabia. The news that Chancellor Angela Merkel and Germany&rsquo;s security council had signed off on the shipment of state-of-the-art tanks to the Middle East after the Saudis helped suppress the uprising in Bahrain led to an uproar this week and to the failed attempt by left-wing parties to scuttle arms shipments through legislation in the Bundestag. Mrs. Merkel, who also leads the security council, has refused to confirm or deny that a deal is in place, citing legal requirements to keep all the council&rsquo;s decisions secret.
