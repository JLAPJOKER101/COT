Heres an interesting little project that, while its unlikely to grow into a major product, nevertheless demonstrates the potential of alternative interfaces. Bruno Zamborlins Mogees (an abbreviation of mosaicing gestural surface) takes input from a contact microphone and analyzes it to determine the placement and direction of gestures on any surface through which vibrations can be detected.I wrote a while back about how the finger on a glass touchscreen wasnt the be-all and end-all of user interaction. The stylus, for example, has much life left in it. And interfaces we havent even thought of will emerge as well.
