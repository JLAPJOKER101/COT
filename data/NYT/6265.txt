

A new regional group setting itself up as a counterweight to the United States in the Western Hemisphere began a two-day summit meeting in Caracas on Friday. Unlike the Organization of American States, the Community of Latin American and Caribbean States includes Cuba and excludes the United States and Canada. President Hugo Ch&aacute;vez of Venezuela and President Daniel Ortega of Nicaragua said it was time for the region to end the Monroe Doctrine, the policy of United States intervention in the hemisphere declared by President James Monroe in 1823. &ldquo;We are sentencing the Monroe Doctrine to death,&rdquo; Mr. Ortega said before the opening ceremony.
