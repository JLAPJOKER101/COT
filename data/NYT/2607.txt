

A former worker at a rural credit cooperative in northwest China lighted a bottle full of gasoline and threw it into a meeting of his former colleagues on Friday morning, injuring 49 people, 19 of them seriously, the Chinese government said in a statement. The suspect was identified as Yang Xianwen, who fled after starting the fire about 8:30 a.m., the statement said. He was captured by the police at 5 p.m. Mr. Yang had been fired from the cooperative for embezzling public funds, the statement said, without explaining whether the money had belonged to the cooperative or another public entity. The incident occurred in Wuwei City in Gansu Province.
