

The police chief in Lahore said Thursday that an American development expert kidnapped there two weeks ago had been freed, only to retract the statement hours later. The police chief, Malik Ahmed Raza Tahir, initially said the police had found Warren Weinstein, left, in Khushab, 125 miles northwest of Lahore, and freed him early Thursday. Several hours later, he backtracked, saying that Mr. Weinstein had not been recovered. He offered no further explanation. Mr. Weinstein, 70, the Pakistan country director for a Virginia-based development consulting company, was kidnapped from his home in Lahore on Aug. 13.
