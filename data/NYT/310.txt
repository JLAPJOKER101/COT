

The Irish prime minister, Brian Cowen, won a confidence vote among lawmakers for the governing Fianna Fail party on Tuesday, virtually assuring that he will lead the party into a general election that is expected to take place by the end of March. Uncertainty over the party leadership has grown since the Cowen government accepted harsh terms in December for a $113-billion international bailout of Ireland&rsquo;s debt-ridden economy.  After the ballot, Mr. Cowen accepted the resignation of Micheal Martin, the foreign minister, who had demanded over the weekend that Mr. Cowen resign.
