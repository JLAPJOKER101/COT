

Parliament on Wednesday declared illegal the so-called silent protests that have swept across the former Soviet republic in the last few months as it struggles to overcome a financial crisis. The demonstrations began after Belarus, trying to curb a large current account deficit, devalued its ruble by 36 percent in May, pushing consumer prices up. President Aleksandr G. Lukashenko, in power since 1994, described such rallies as part of a plot to overthrow his government.
