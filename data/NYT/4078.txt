

Afghan police said at least three people were killed by a car bomb at a hotel in northern Afghanistan, which has seen a rise in insurgent attacks. A spokesman for the governor of Kunduz Province said that shortly after dawn Tuesday, a car bomber blew up his vehicle outside the hotel, which is frequented by foreigners. Two other men rushed inside and engaged the police in a two-hour gun battle. he said. Three guards working at the guest house, in the city of Kunduz, were killed and 10 other people were wounded.
