

A senior Indonesian figure in Al Qaeda wanted in the 2002 bombing of a Bali nightclub has been arrested in Pakistan, intelligence sources in Jakarta said Tuesday. Few details were released about the suspect, Umar Patek, who is accused of having helped assemble bombs that were used in the attack, which killed 202 people, including seven Americans. Mr. Patek is suspected of having served in the Qaeda-linked militant group Jemaah Islamiyah. His whereabouts were not revealed.
