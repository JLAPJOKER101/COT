

Syrian forces killed five people on Monday in raids to shut down the antigovernment protest movement, the Syrian Observatory for Human Rights reported. The group, which is based in London, said the raids were conducted in Houla, an area that consists of several villages in central Homs Province. The United Nations human rights office, meanwhile, said that Syrian security forces had killed at least 100 people in the past week. The death toll during the six months of unrest is now more than 2,700 people, said Kang Kyung, the United Nations&rsquo; deputy high commissioner for human rights.
