

A sensational story of sex, cocaine and tabloid wrongdoing, as told by a woman who used to run an escort service, has revived questions over the relationship between The News of the World, the newspaper shut by Rupert Murdoch in the phone hacking scandal, and George Osborne, the chancellor of the Exchequer. The former escort boss, Natalie Rowe, contended in a television interview broadcast on Monday that the newspaper deliberately twisted her assertion six years ago that she and Mr. Osborne used to snort cocaine together years ago so that Mr. Osborne would not be tainted.
