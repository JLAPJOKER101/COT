

The Royal Navy confirmed on Tuesday that it had appointed the first woman in its history to command a warship. Lt. Cmdr. Sarah West, 39, will take up her post on the HMS Portland, a frigate, in April. Lieutenant Commander West&rsquo;s previous naval experience includes serving in the Persian Gulf on the Middle East operations team. Women remain barred from serving in the Royal Marines, the Submarine Service, the Household Cavalry and the Royal Armored Corps.
