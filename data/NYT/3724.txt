

Hundreds of angry marchers forced their way into the grounds of the Cypriot presidential palace in Nicosia late Tuesday to protest the naval base explosion that killed 12 people, wrecked a power plant and forced the resignations of the defense minister and the top military chief. Many parts of the island were without power because the explosion knocked out the main power station. The authorities have ruled out sabotage, but many Cypriots have criticized how the containers that exploded &mdash; most of them filled with gunpowder &mdash; had been stored.
