

Fourteen countries, including the United States, vowed Friday to work to promote online freedom, with an emphasis on helping bloggers who operate under oppressive governments. The countries, which included Britain, France and Canada, endorsed a statement at a conference in The Hague saying that their goals included preventing &ldquo;the misappropriation of technologies for repressive ends, inappropriate requests for personal data for political purposes, and illegitimate blocking of content.&rdquo; The conference was inspired in part by the bloggers and social network users who helped foment the revolutions of the Arab Spring. The signers did not include any Arab or Middle Eastern governments.
