

Tajik security forces have killed an Islamist warlord and 10 of his fighters who had been accused of an attack on a column of government troops, Tajik news agencies reported Friday. The Islamic Movement of Uzbekistan, which is linked to Al Qaeda, had claimed responsibility for the Sept. 19 attack on government troops in which 28 soldiers were killed. The government launched a retaliatory strike against Islamist rebels in September. On Friday, ASIA-Plus news agency reported that Abdullo Rakhimov, better known as Mullah Abdullo, and 10 of his supporters, had been killed in eastern Tajikistan by Tajik secret services. At least two security service officers died in the operation, it said.
