

Paraguay&rsquo;s opposition-controlled Congress rejected on Thursday a constitutional reform that would have let the leftist President Fernando Lugo run for re-election. The reform drive, led by Mr. Lugo&rsquo;s supporters, proved controversial in a country where many people have painful memories of Gen. Alfredo Stroessner&rsquo;s 35-year dictatorship. Mr. Lugo&rsquo;s allies last month presented a petition to Congress signed by 90,000 people, urging lawmakers to amend the Constitution, which has limited leaders to a single term since 1992.
