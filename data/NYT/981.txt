

Negotiators for the Philippine government and Communist rebels agreed Monday on a road map for continued peace talks aimed at resolving one of Asia&rsquo;s longest-running conflicts by June 2012. Ending the first round of  peace negotiations in Nesbru, Norway, the two sides agreed to meet again in April and every two months after that. Both sides declared a cease-fire during the week of talks, their first formal peace negotiations in six years.
