

The United States will give $105 million in aid to alleviate hunger in Somalia, the White House said Monday. The White House spokesman, Jay Carney, said the assistance would come from &ldquo;existing pools of money.&rdquo; The United States has earmarked $500 million for famine relief efforts in the Horn of Africa, a region that includes Somalia. About 3.6 million people are at risk of starvation in Somalia, the United Nations says.
