

Both sides in the convoluted legal battle over Mikhail B. Khodorkovsky&rsquo;s now-defunct Yukos Oil were claiming victory on Tuesday after the European Court of Human Rights ruled that Russia had violated the rights of company representatives to a fair trial, but failed to find evidence that Russian authorities had intentionally misused legal proceedings to bankrupt and dismantle the company. Yukos representatives had accused the Russian government of applying tax laws selectively in an effort to assume control of the company after Mr. Khodorkovsky was arrested in 2003.
